Rental Investment
Metrics Guide

Understanding the numbers behind every deal — from cap rate to DSCR and beyond.

Return Metrics

Cap Rate

The capitalization rate shows your annual return if you bought the property all cash. Calculated as NOI ÷ Purchase Price.

≥7% Excellent  ·  5–7% Good  ·  <5% Review carefully

Cash-on-Cash Return

Measures annual cash flow vs. the actual cash you invested (down payment + closing + rehab). Formula: Annual Cash Flow ÷ Total Cash Invested.

≥8% Excellent  ·  5–8% Good  ·  <5% Thin margins

Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)

A quick filter: Purchase Price ÷ Annual Gross Rent. Lower is better — how many years of rent to cover the price.

≤10x Strong  ·  10–15x Moderate  ·  >15x Expensive
Risk Metrics

DSCR — Debt Service Coverage Ratio

How well your NOI covers the mortgage: NOI ÷ Annual Debt Service. Lenders typically require ≥1.25. Below 1.0 means negative cash flow.

≥1.3x Comfortable  ·  1.0–1.3x Tight  ·  <1.0x Negative cash flow

Break-Even Ratio

The percentage of income needed to cover all expenses + debt: (OpEx + Debt Service) ÷ Gross Income. Lower means you can absorb more vacancy before going underwater.

≤75% Safe buffer  ·  75–90% Watch closely  ·  >90% Risky
5-Year Projection

PropCompare models three wealth-building components over 5 years:

  • Cash Flow — cumulative net income after all expenses and mortgage
  • Equity Paydown — principal reduction on your loan each year
  • Appreciation — estimated value gain at your input appreciation rate

Projections are estimates. Not financial advice.
